Autobiographical Game

A simple autobiographical narrative game built with p5.js, using objects, collision detection, and scene switching to explore memory, movement, and personal experience.

Sketch

Description & Reflection

Surviving from the Dream is a short interactive game inspired by my personal experiences with vivid dreams and sleep paralysis. Sometimes, I feel trapped inside a dream and notice that I return to similar dream scenarios repeatedly. This project started from that feeling, and I wanted to translate it into a playable experience rather than a linear story.

The game begins as the player falls asleep and moves through two dream worlds: an endless corridor and a quiet underwater space. In the first dream, the player must run, jump, and choose the correct door to move forward. In the second dream, the player must stay inside a moving light circle long enough to fill a gauge and wake up. Failing either challenge causes the dream to loop, reflecting how dreams can feel inescapable until a certain moment of awareness.

During testing, I realized how hard it was to balance the difficulty. Small changes in timing or collision size could quickly make the game feel either frustrating or too easy. I spent a lot of time tuning these details so the interaction would feel gentle but still engaging, without breaking the dream-like feeling.
