Assignment 7 – Autobiographical Game

p5.js

A simple autobiographical narrative game built with p5.js, using objects, collision detection, and scene switching to explore memory, movement, and personal experience.

Sketch

Description & Reflection

This project started from my interest in dreams as places you move through, rather than stories you control. I wanted to make a simple game where the player drifts between different dream-like spaces, each with its own mood and rules.

I planned the game as a series of scenes, including a bedroom, a corridor, an underwater space, and a waking moment. I used a scene variable and slow fades to move between them, so the experience would feel smooth instead of sudden. In p5.js, I used simple shapes, particles, and light to keep the visuals emotional and slightly unclear, like a dream.

The interaction is very simple. There is no clear win or goal. Instead, the player learns how to move with the environment, such as staying inside moving light. I adjusted speed, brightness, and timing many times to keep the experience calm, not stressful.

During testing, I realized how hard it was to balance the difficulty. Small changes in timing or collision size could quickly make the game feel either frustrating or too easy. I spent a lot of time tuning these details so the interaction would feel gentle but still engaging, without breaking the dream-like feeling.
