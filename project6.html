Data Portrait

A circular portrait of when I checked the clock over six days, with dots that glow as a moving line passes, turning a small daily habit into a calm data visualization.

Sketch

Description & Reflection

For my Data Portrait, I tracked how often and when I check the clock during the day. I recorded the exact times for six days. At first, it felt like a random habit, but I realized it shows how I pay attention and how my daily rhythm changes.

Each ring represents one day, and each dot is the time I looked at the clock. The white line moves around like a hand on a clock, and when it passes near a dot, that dot glows for a moment. Brighter areas mean I checked the time more often around that hour. I collected the data by writing down the times by hand whenever I intentionally checked the time, and then converted them into minutes and angles so I could place them around circles.

At first I wasn’t sure how I wanted to visualize my data, but I knew I wanted all six days to be visible at once so I could compare them easily. I was inspired by my earlier “personal clock” idea and the Dear Data project, and decided to build a clock-like diagram that shows the moments I checked the time. I made six rings in different colors and a simple moving hand that makes dots glow when it gets close, keeping the overall feeling quiet and clear.

While doing this, I noticed I check the clock a lot when I’m waiting for something, like during workouts or when I want the day to end faster. It made me aware that even small, everyday actions can become interesting data and say something about how I experience time.
