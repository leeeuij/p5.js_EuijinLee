Assignment 3 – Brain Time Clock

p5.js

A two-layer clock where personal time only moves when the cursor rests calmly in the center circle, showing the gap between time that passes and time I actually inhabit.

Sketch

Description & Reflection

This clock shows two timelines side by side: STANDARD TIME (elapsed since launch) and PERSONAL TIME (time I actually inhabit). PERSONAL TIME advances only when I sit inside the center circle and keep the cursor still for two seconds. When I am calm, a clean ring fills and gentle circles expand outward. When I am rushing or fidgeting, noisy waves appear and PERSONAL TIME freezes. The monochrome look keeps the rule readable without distraction.

I started with three brainstorms: a Stillness Clock that only advances when the mouse is still, a Focus Circle Clock that tracks time only inside a central circle, and an Energy Budget Clock that spends down a daily energy bar. While prototyping, I noticed a real habit: when I have too many tasks, time feels like it’s racing, and if I pause briefly to list tasks, breathe, or close my eyes, time feels normal again. That insight led me to merge the stillness gate with the focus circle.

Translating this into code taught me to think in milliseconds with millis() and deltaTime, to use map() to turn data into angles and ring lengths, and to use noise() for motion that reads as disorder vs. order. While using the clock, I can literally see PERSONAL TIME freeze when I rush, which makes me stop and re-enter the center on purpose. In the end it visualizes time the way my mind experiences it: calm flows, chaos freezes.
