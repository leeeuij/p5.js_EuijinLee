Optical Illusion

p5.js

A rotating spiral illusion made from nested loops, speeding up on mouse press to create a stronger motion aftereffect inspired by the “waterfall illusion.”

Sketch

Process & Reflection

I started by researching different types of motion illusions and was drawn to the “waterfall illusion,” where watching movement for a while makes the eye see opposite motion afterward. I wanted to recreate that feeling with simple shapes and loops. Using for and nested for loops, I built rings made of rectangles that rotate together like a spiral.

I added an if/else condition so the pattern speeds up when the mouse is pressed. I tested different speeds to find a pace that feels natural – not too fast to break the illusion, but fast enough to create a strong motion effect. I adjusted the distance between rings and the angle offset until the flow looked smooth and hypnotic.

I imagine this illusion as an interactive element on a website or museum installation where people can experience motion aftereffects in real time. It could also live as a background animation or printed pattern on fabric that suggests movement when the wearer walks.
